About Me

I'm Luke Herbeck, an MBA candidate at Texas A&M's Mays Business School with an engineering foundation in Industrial Distribution. After graduating in May 2026, I'll be joining SourceBlue as a Supply Chain Coordinator, where I'll focus on project coordination and supplier management in the construction industry.

What drew me to this field is the tangible impact. In construction, performance hinges on tight coordination: getting the right materials to the right site at the right time, even when schedules shift. That challenge excites me because it demands both analytical rigor and the ability to communicate clearly across teams, suppliers, and stakeholders.

My background bridges two worlds. My engineering degree taught me to understand systems, analyze data, and optimize processes. My MBA sharpened my ability to think strategically, lead cross-functional teams, and connect operational details with executive-level priorities. This combination allows me to see both the granular realities and the bigger picture, and translate between them effectively.

I'm strongest when I can bring structure to ambiguity. Whether that means building a forecasting model to project long-term costs, redesigning a workflow to eliminate inefficiencies, or coordinating multiple stakeholders toward a shared deadline, I thrive on turning complexity into clarity.

What motivates me is seeing systems improve and watching that improvement ripple through real operations. I'm at my best when I can help a team understand what's happening, what it costs, what to prioritize, and how to deliver.
